About

Vani Subramony (she/her) is a recent graduate of Case Western Reserve University with a BA in Cognitive Science and Philosophy, and a minor in Applied Data Science. During her time at CWRU, she served as the General Manager and a programmer at WRUW-FM, the university's college radio station. She also served as a News Editor for The Case Western Reserve Observer, CWRU's student-run publication. Additionally, she has worked as a producer and freelance journalist at WNIJ, the NPR affiliate station in Dekalb, Illinois.

Vani's journey as a radio programmer and reporter, print journalist and advocate for free speech has taught her how to document historically transformative moments with factual rigor and empathy. She hopes to continue channeling her passion into telling impactful stories that influence social change.

Vani is currently pursuing her Master of Science in Journalism in Northwestern University's Medill School of Journalism.
